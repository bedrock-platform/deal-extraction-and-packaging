## GARM Risk Rating Scale

GARM (Global Alliance for Responsible Media) defines four risk rating levels:

- **Floor**: Lowest risk level - content suitable for all advertisers
  - Family-friendly content, no controversial topics
  - Mainstream, brand-safe inventory
  - Examples: Major broadcast networks (ABC, CBS, NBC), family entertainment, educational content, streaming platforms (Disney+, Netflix, Hulu)

- **Low**: Low risk - suitable for most advertisers
  - Generally brand-safe content
  - Minor or contextual references to sensitive topics
  - Mainstream publishers and platforms
  - Examples: News sites, lifestyle content, general entertainment, established publishers (CNN, BBC, Reuters)

- **Medium**: Medium risk - requires careful consideration
  - Some controversial or sensitive content
  - May not be suitable for all verticals
  - Requires brand-specific evaluation
  - Examples: Opinion content, some news commentary, mature themes (not explicit), some user-generated content platforms

- **High**: High risk - not suitable for most advertisers
  - Explicit or controversial content
  - Violent, adult, or harmful content
  - Not brand-safe for most verticals
  - Examples: Adult content, extremist content, graphic violence, illegal content, hate speech

## Assessment Guidelines

Assess GARM risk rating based on:

1. **Publisher Reputation**: 
   - Known brand-safe publishers (Disney, ESPN, CNN, etc.) → Low/Floor
   - Major broadcast networks → Low/Floor
   - Established publishers → Low
   - Unknown or unverified publishers → Medium/High

2. **Content Type**:
   - Family entertainment → Floor
   - Mainstream news (including political news from reputable publishers) → Low, family_safe=True
   - General entertainment → Low
   - Opinion/commentary from reputable sources → Low/Medium, family_safe=True
   - Extremist political content, unmoderated UGC → Medium/High, family_safe=False
   - Adult, violent, extremist → High, family_safe=False

3. **Inventory Type**:
   - CTV from major platforms (Disney+, Netflix, Hulu) → Floor/Low
   - CTV from premium publishers → Low
   - Standard web display → Low/Medium
   - User-generated content platforms → Medium/High

4. **Deal Characteristics**:
   - Higher floor price often indicates premium inventory → Lower risk
   - Deal name keywords (Premium, Brand-Safe, Verified) → Lower risk
   - Deal description indicating quality → Lower risk

## Family-Safe Flag

- **True**: Content suitable for all audiences, including children
  - No adult content, violence, or graphic imagery
  - Mainstream news coverage (including politics, current events) from reputable publishers
  - General entertainment, lifestyle, and educational content
  - Established publishers (New York Post, CNN, BBC, Reuters, etc.) covering news/politics
  - Suitable for CPG, family brands, education verticals
  
  **Key Point**: Mainstream political news from reputable publishers is family-safe. Only extremist, violent, or explicit content disqualifies family-safe status.

- **False**: May contain adult content, mature themes, or extremist/controversial content
  - Explicit adult content, graphic violence, hate speech
  - Extremist political content (not mainstream news coverage)
  - User-generated content platforms with unmoderated controversial content
  - Not suitable for family brands or sensitive verticals
  
  **Key Point**: Do NOT mark as False simply because content includes "Politics" in taxonomy. Mainstream news publishers covering political topics should be True unless there's evidence of extremist, violent, or explicit content.

## Safe-for-Verticals Assessment

Based on GARM risk rating, determine which verticals are safe:

- **Floor/Low Risk**: Safe for all verticals including Automotive, Finance, Retail, Technology, Travel, CPG, Health, Education, Entertainment
- **Medium Risk**: Limited verticals (may exclude sensitive brands like CPG, Education)
- **High Risk**: Very limited or no verticals safe

## Aggregation Rules (for future package creation)

When combining multiple deals into packages:
- Package Risk Rating = MAX(Deal Risk Ratings) - Use most restrictive rating (worst-case approach)
- Family Safe = ALL deals must be family-safe (if any deal is false, package is false)

   - Only mark False if there's evidence of extremist, violent, explicit, or unmoderated controversial content
   - Established publishers (NY Post, CNN, BBC, Reuters, etc.) covering news/politics should be True
   - Do NOT mark False simply because taxonomy includes "Politics" - distinguish mainstream news from extremist content
4. List safe-for-verticals based on risk rating assessment.
